## Comparative Analysis

The fundamental challenge of prebiotic **Valine** synthesis is its **branched isobutyl skeleton**. Linear carbon chain elongation is relatively easy in origins-of-life chemistry; creating specific, deoxygenated branches is incredibly difficult. 

This distinct challenge cleanly separates the networks into three tiers:
1. **The Chemically Accurate (Config E):** Config E is the only network that explicitly and correctly solves the branching problem. By starting with acetone (which already contains a branch-like central carbon) and utilizing step-by-step cyanosulfidic reductive homologation (via cyanohydrins and thioamides), it builds the C5 skeleton with perfect mass balance and regiochemistry.
2. **The Abstractors (Configs C & A):** These configs understand that the branching is hard, but they attempt to sidestep it. Config C blacks-boxes the difficult steps into single abstractions (which is chemically safe, though lacking in detail). Config A attempts to specify a mechanism (acetaldehyde to isobutyraldehyde) but gets the fundamental organic chemistry wrong, as an aldol of two C2 molecules yields a linear C4 chain, not a branched one.
3. **The Chemically Broken (Configs F, B, & D):** These networks fail at the most basic level of chemical accounting. They either magically generate 5-carbon molecules from 1-carbon inputs (Config B), turn C3 molecules into C5 molecules without adding carbon (Config F), or propose wildly fabricated reactions that violate the fundamental rules of functional group chemistry (Config D).
