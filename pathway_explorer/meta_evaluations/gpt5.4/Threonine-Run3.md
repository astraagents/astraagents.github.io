### Comparative Analysis

The highest-performing configurations (**A, D, and C**) succeeded by anchoring their networks in the meticulously validated cyanosulfidic photoreduction mechanisms developed by the Sutherland lab (where HCN and cyanohydrins are selectively reduced by UV/H₂S/Cu to lactaldehyde). 

**Config A** took the top spot because it recognized a critical structural problem—threonine's chemical instability—and provided robust prebiotic solutions: converting it into peptides and utilizing the Bucherer-Bergs pathway to capture precursors as stable hydantoins. **Config D** achieved a close second by successfully integrating state-of-the-art alternative systems chemistry (Krishnamurthy's DAP-mediated phosphoro-Strecker). **Config C** was the most rigorously accurate to experimental bench conditions but lacked the broad systems-level creativity of A and D.

Conversely, the lower-scoring configurations stumbled on precursor selectivity and experimental validity. **Config E** relied on the notoriously messy formose reaction to generate its C3 aldehydes, while **Config F** completely abandoned established prebiotic literature to invent a speculative, enzyme-free "biological mimicry" pathway that would likely fail in a real prebiotic soup.
