## Comparative Analysis
The defining feature separating the top-tier networks (C, E, B, A) from the lower-tier networks (D, F) is their **avoidance of the retro-biosynthetic trap**. Config F fails completely because it assumes modern biological pathways (forward TCA cycle) can operate cleanly without enzymes. 

The top configs recognize that prebiotic chemistry must utilize distinct proto-metabolic pathways (like glyoxylate/pyruvate condensations or cyanosulfidic homologation) to reach modern hubs like α-ketoglutarate. 

**Config C takes the #1 spot** because it behaves like a true organic chemist evaluating a messy system. Instead of ignoring cyanide's tendency to poison carbonyls, it explicitly utilizes cyanohydrin formation as a chemical storage mechanism. It also acknowledges the reality of chemical selectivity by mapping the competitive formation of hydroxyketoglutarate alongside glutamate during reduction. **Config E is a very close runner-up**, serving as a flawless, highly granular transcription of the most successful recent origins-of-life laboratory experiments.
