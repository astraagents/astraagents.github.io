## Comparative Analysis
The primary differentiator separating the top-tier configurations (F and A) from the rest is their ability to construct a **fully connected, bottom-up network graph** that strictly utilizes the permitted simple precursors. Config F edges out Config A by solving a difficult chemical bottleneck: rather than forcing the speculative fragmentation of pyruvate to get glyoxylate (as A does), Config F generates glyoxylate naturally via surface sugar (formose) oxidation. Config F then brilliantly uses this glyoxylate in a transamination reaction with alanine—which it also built from scratch—creating a realistic proto-metabolic cycle.

A systematic pattern of failure is evident in the lower-ranked configurations (C and E). They suffer from "orphan nodes"—utilizing highly complex intermediates as starting materials without providing a synthetic pathway to create them. Config E is completely inverted, relying on the degradation of complex amino acids to make glycine, which is prebiotically backward. Config D highlights a different failure mode: hallucinating a thermodynamically implausible reaction (HCHO + NH3 -> HCN) simply to force graph connectivity, which undermines the chemical validity of the subsequent pathways.
