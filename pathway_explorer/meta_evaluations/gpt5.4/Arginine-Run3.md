## Comparative Analysis

The evaluation of these networks highlights the tension in prebiotic arginine synthesis: whether to follow the experimentally proven but biologically unfamiliar **cyanosulfidic route** (Patel et al., 2015), or attempt to prebiotically reverse-engineer the biological **Urea Cycle** (Glutamate -> Ornithine -> Arginine).

**Config B** takes the top spot because it recognizes that the answer to this tension is *both*. It flawlessly maps the non-biological cyanosulfidic synthesis to generate the first arginine monomers, but also integrates the latest research (Longo et al., 2020) showing that early peptides containing ornithine could be post-synthetically guanidinated to form arginine residues. This captures the absolute cutting edge of systems chemistry.

**Config E** and **Config A** take the 2nd and 3rd spots by relying strictly on empirical data. Config E's mapping of specific cyclic intermediates and thioamide loops is chemically stunning, while Config A provides a great macro-level view of the literature. 

**Config F** and **Config D** attempt the biological reverse-engineering route. Config D fails because it uses generic, thermodynamically impossible steps to get from pyruvate to ornithine. **Config F**, however, succeeds as a theoretical exercise by proposing specific prebiotic activating agents (cyanamide, cyanate) to bypass the lack of ATP in the Urea Cycle. While empirically lacking, Config F demonstrates exceptional chemical creativity, distinguishing the top-tier configs from the bottom-tier.
