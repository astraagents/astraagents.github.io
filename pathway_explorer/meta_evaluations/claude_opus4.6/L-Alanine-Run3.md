## Comparative Analysis

The primary differentiator among these networks is how they handle the classical bottlenecks of prebiotic amino acid synthesis: **aminonitrile stability** and **reductant availability**. 

**Config A** is the clear winner because it incorporates the latest breakthroughs (Pulletikurti 2022) to bypass aminonitrile degradation entirely by trapping it as a hydantoin (Bucherer-Bergs pathway). Furthermore, A seamlessly integrates realistic H₂-driven reductive amination (Moran 2024) using native metals, creating a bridge to biological dehydrogenases. 

**Config E** takes the second spot through sheer structural rigor. Many prebiotic models that utilize Muchowska's iron-promoted metabolic network ignore the fact that hydroxylamine is difficult to accumulate. Config E elegantly solves this by modeling volcanic NO reduction on Fe⁰. Furthermore, E rigorously avoids the "circularity trap" of using metabolic intermediates to synthesize themselves.

**Config C** was highly competitive for the top spot due to its brilliant use of formamide as a stabilizing agent for aminonitriles (Green 2023) and its proto-enzymatic metal-PLP transamination (Dherbassy 2023). However, it slipped to third because it imports Pyridoxal (a complex heterocyclic cofactor) without modeling its synthesis, whereas Config A models all reactants from C1/C2 bases.

Configs **B, F, and D** represent progressively more traditional views of prebiotic chemistry. **B** differentiates itself with a fascinating—if chronologically ambitious—narrative regarding chiral selection via pyrite and RNA. **F** is a perfectly sound, textbook network but lacks the modern "tricks" to overcome yield limits. **D** ranks last primarily because it leans on the formose-ammonia reaction, which is notoriously messy and yields low selectivities compared to the highly targeted catalytic pathways deployed by the top three networks.
