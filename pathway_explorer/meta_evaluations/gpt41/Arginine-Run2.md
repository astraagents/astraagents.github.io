## Comparative Analysis

The fundamental separator in this evaluation was **carbon counting and functional group transformations**. Arginine is a 6-carbon molecule with a 5-carbon continuous backbone. The primary cyanosulfidic starting materials (acrylonitrile, $\beta$-aminopropionitrile) contain only a 3-carbon backbone. 

Configs A, B, and C all recognized the cyanosulfidic literature but failed to understand the underlying organic chemistry. They attempted to slap a cyanamide and an HCN onto a 3-carbon chain, which mathematically yields a 4-carbon backbone (yielding an analog like 2-amino-4-guanidinobutyric acid), completely failing to synthesize arginine. 

Configs D and F attempted to use a 5-carbon biological precursor (glutamate) but failed to understand how to chemically convert it. Config D claimed decarboxylation could yield ornithine (which actually yields 4-carbon GABA), and Config F claimed peptide bonds could build a carbon backbone.

**Config E** stands miles above the rest because it correctly models the exact, iterative organic chemistry required to build the molecule. It accurately details the pyrimidine ring cyclization, the subsequent HCN-driven ring-opening (adding the 4th carbon), the thioamide reduction, and the second cyanohydrin/Strecker homologation (adding the 5th carbon). Config E proves that in prebiotic chemistry, network topology is meaningless if the fundamental stoichiometry and valency do not add up.
