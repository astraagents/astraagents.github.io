## Comparative Analysis
The configurations separate cleanly into three tiers based on their adherence to the fundamental laws of chemistry and current literature. 

**Tier 1 (Configs E and C)** demonstrates profound expertise in prebiotic chemistry. Config E is the standout winner due to its absolute mechanistic precision; it perfectly captures the Patel et al. (2015) cyanosulfidic pathway, including the highly specific thiolation and reductive deoxygenation steps that most models overlook. Config C is a close second, offering a broader, multi-environmental approach that brilliantly incorporates cutting-edge astrochemical ice photochemistry (Simionato 2018) and wet-dry prolinamide cycling (Guo 2024). 

**Tier 2 (Configs A and B)** features strong conceptual frameworks that are let down by specific chemical errors. Config A struggles with the thermodynamics of reducing free carboxylates and forces a Strecker reaction onto DAMN. Config B is conceptually beautiful in its linking of hydrothermal and surface environments, but commits a fatal chemistry sin by labeling the conversion of a carboxylic acid to an aldehyde as a "partial oxidation/dehydrogenation"—a blatant misunderstanding of redox states. 

**Tier 3 (Configs D and F)** fails the most basic tenets of stoichiometry and mass balance. Config D proposes that betaine (trimethylglycine) can cyclize into a pyrrolidine ring, completely ignoring the impossibility of mapping three isolated methyl groups into a continuous carbon ring. Config F proposes that C2 glycine becomes C5 $\alpha$-ketoglutarate via simple UV exposure, entirely ignoring where the extra three carbon atoms come from. These models hallucinate chemical pathways rather than constructing them.
