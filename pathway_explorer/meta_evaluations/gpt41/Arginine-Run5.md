## Comparative Analysis

The clear dividing line in this evaluation was the ability to handle the specific, highly complex chemical reality of prebiotic arginine synthesis. Because arginine requires a 5-carbon chain ending in a highly specific guanidino group, generic "Strecker" or "hydrothermal" hand-waving fails immediately. 

**Config E** separated itself from the pack by perfectly capturing the counter-intuitive but experimentally proven reality of this synthesis: you must guanylate a short-chain nitrile *first*, cyclize it into a pyrimidine ring, and then use HCN and H₂S to open the ring and extend the chain. Config E nailed every mechanistic detail of this.

**Config B** took a strong second place by acknowledging a major alternative theory: arginine was likely a late evolutionary addition formed by the post-synthetic modification of ornithine already incorporated into early peptides (using amyloid templates). 

The remaining configs suffered from varying degrees of chemical illiteracy. **Configs A and C** tried to describe the cyanosulfidic pathway but botched the intermediate steps and reagents (omitting sulfur entirely or mixing up cyanate/cyanamide). **Configs D and F** attempted to force biological metabolic pathways (like the urea cycle) into prebiotic environments, resulting in completely impossible reactions—such as using inert guanidine as a reagent, or inventing a one-step transamination from a keto-acid directly to a diamino acid.
