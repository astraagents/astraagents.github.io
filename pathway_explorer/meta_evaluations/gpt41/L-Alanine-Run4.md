## Comparative Analysis

The clear dividing line among these networks is **chemical rigor combined with literature currency**. 

**Config C** and **Config E** represent the apex of the field, utilizing deeply specific, recent literature to construct networks that solve actual kinetic and thermodynamic roadblocks. Config C takes the top spot because it successfully bridges strict abiotic geochemistry (native Ni amination, deep vent C-fixation) with the origins of biochemistry (pyridoxal-metal transamination), effectively modeling the transition from geochemical to biological metabolism. Config E is equally rigorous but narrower in scope, focusing strictly on a pyruvate-hub proto-metabolic topology.

**Configs A and B** are both excellent, highly plausible models. Config A is more ambitious, bringing in the Bucherer-Bergs pathway, which bypasses the hydrolytic instability of aminonitriles—a known issue in prebiotic chemistry. Config B relies on safer, classic textbook models (Miller-Urey, standard Sutherland chemistry) but executes them perfectly.

The bottom tier is defined by **chemical impossibility**. **Config D** attempts to use formamide in a Cannizzaro-type reaction and misapplies glycolonitrile chemistry, resulting in implausible surface pathways. **Config F** fails entirely on basic carbon-counting; it attempts to perform cyanation (which adds a carbon) on 3-carbon precursors (pyruvate) and 2-carbon sugars (glycolaldehyde) while expecting 3-carbon target backbones (alanine), a fundamental stoichiometric failure.
