## Comparative Analysis
The defining feature separating the top-ranked configs (E, C, A) from the bottom-ranked configs (D, F) is **chemical and thermodynamic realism**. Arginine is arguably the most difficult canonical amino acid to synthesize prebiotically due to its long carbon chain and the highly basic guanidino group. 

**Config E** stands out as a masterclass in prebiotic systems chemistry. Instead of treating chain homologation as a black box, it explicitly maps the actual literature-supported mechanism: forming a pyrimidine ring from cyanamide, hydrolyzing it, and using HCN to pop the ring open to extend the carbon chain. This level of mechanistic rigor is unmatched in the other configs.

**Configs A, B, and C** all lean heavily on the Patel et al. (2015) cyanosulfidic network, which is the current gold standard for prebiotic arginine synthesis. Config A distinguishes itself by successfully integrating an older, but highly plausible, alternative route (direct guanylation of ornithine by cyanamide). 

**Configs D and F** represent common pitfalls in prebiotic network generation. Config F assumes that biology's current pathway (the urea cycle) can run abiotically without accounting for the fact that biology uses ATP to overcome the massive thermodynamic barriers of amide amination and carboxylic acid reduction. Config D fails chemically by assuming free guanidine can act as a guanylating agent, ignoring its resonance stabilization, and relying on "magic" steps to jump from simple C1 compounds to C5 backbones.
