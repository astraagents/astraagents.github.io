## Comparative Analysis

The evaluation of these six networks highlights a sharp divide based on **network contiguity** and **adherence to boundary constraints**. 

**The Top Tier (Configs A and B)** correctly understood the assignment of bottom-up synthesis. They successfully trace unbroken, multi-environment pathways from the permitted C1/N1 starting materials all the way to Glycine. Config A edges out Config B slightly on chemical fundamentals; while Config B's architectural mapping is flawless, it commits a grave chemical hallucination by claiming the formose reaction synthesizes formaldehyde from CO₂ and water. Config A avoids major hallucinations and successfully integrates very recent literature (e.g., 2024 ferroan brucite studies).

**The Middle Tier (Configs C and D)** features chemically interesting ideas but fails in structural execution. Config C introduces brilliant, highly novel computational pathways (direct silicate catalysis) but shoots itself in the foot by violating the prompt constraints, treating complex C2 molecules (ethanolamine) as simple starting materials. Config D adheres to the starting materials but proposes a physically absurd geographical flow, requiring molecules to travel from the surface to the deep sea and back again multiple times just to complete a single pathway.

**The Bottom Tier (Configs F and E)** suffers from "magic intermediate syndrome." Config F proposes interesting transamination reactions but forgets to actually synthesize the complex amines it relies on. Config E represents a complete conceptual failure for an origin-of-life buildup network; instead of synthesizing Glycine from simple molecules, it relies on the top-down thermal degradation of vastly more complex amino acids (like Serine and Threonine) that it illegitimately uses as starting materials.
