## Comparative Analysis

The fundamental differentiator among these networks is **how they solve the kinetic and thermodynamic barriers of carbon fixation and amination without enzymes**. 

**Configs C, E, and B** top the rankings because they utilize *specifically prebiotic* workarounds to these barriers. Config C uses diamidophosphate (DAP), ZnS semiconductor photochemistry, and geoelectrochemistry to drive reactions that would otherwise stall. Config E perfectly leverages the unique properties of iron-promoted aldol condensations and the highly reactive abiotic intermediate hydroxylamine. Config B utilizes high-energy UV photoredox (Sutherland chemistry) to force homologation. These configs recognize that early Earth chemistry likely looked different from modern biochemistry.

**Configs D and F** fall to the bottom because they fall into the "metabolism-first" trap: assuming that modern biochemical pathways (like the reverse TCA cycle and direct transaminations) will naturally proceed on simple mineral surfaces if you just apply enough heat and pressure. The reality of chemical thermodynamics makes abiotic acetate carboxylation or direct transamination exceedingly poor without enzymes. 

**Config A** sits in the middle: it incorporates excellent modern abiotic reactions (like heterogeneous Nickel catalysis) but awkwardly patches in complex biological coenzymes (NADH) to solve difficult reduction steps, bridging the gap between prebiotic and biological chemistry a bit too hastily.
